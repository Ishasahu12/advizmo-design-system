Recommendation

Which concept to use and when.

Recommended

Concept 1: Guided Action List

For the initial release, we recommend Concept 1 (Guided Action List) as the primary implementation. It offers the best balance of scanability, iOS-native feel, and familiarity for users who are accustomed to Apple Settings and Apple Wallet patterns.

The grouped list pattern is deeply ingrained in iOS users' mental models. It requires zero learning curve, supports rapid scanning, and scales gracefully as new actions are added. The section headers ("Accounts", "Cash Flow", "Add Manually") provide natural grouping without requiring an extra navigation step.

Concept 2 (Financial Categories) is an excellent alternative if the action set grows beyond 7-8 items, as the 2-column grid prevents the list from becoming too long. Concept 5 (Financial Assistant) should be considered for premium or onboarding flows where personalization and delight are worth the added complexity.

Concept 1: Guided List

Best for: Quick actions, power users, minimal cognitive load. Use when users need to find and complete actions fast.

Concept 2: Categories

Best for: Larger action sets (7+), new users who need orientation. Use when grouping reduces overwhelm.

Concept 3: Action Cards

Best for: Premium positioning, feature launches. Use when you want actions to feel like products, not settings.

Concept 4: Progressive

Best for: Complex flows with mixed importance. Use when you need to focus attention on the 2-3 most critical actions.

Concept 5: Assistant

Best for: Onboarding, premium tier, high personalization. Use when the app's AI capabilities are a differentiator.
