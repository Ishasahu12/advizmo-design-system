What is tax-loss harvesting?

Tax-loss harvesting is the practice of selling securities at a loss to offset a capital gains tax liability. It is typically used to limit the recognition of short-term capital gains.

Here is how it works in practice:

  • You sell an investment that has lost value
  • The loss offsets gains from other investments
  • You reinvest in a similar but not identical asset

AI Insight: Once your account is set up, I will scan your portfolio daily for harvesting opportunities and alert you when action could save you money.

Concept Comparison

Side-by-side comparison of all 5 concepts to help choose the best approach.

Concept Approach Best For Complexity
Concept 1 Conversational AI Card Direct engagement, chat-first users Medium
Concept 2 AI Assistant with Prompts Guided discovery, new users Medium
Concept 3 Discover Cards Visual learners, engagement High
Concept 4 Interactive Question Chips Playful engagement, quick answers Low
Concept 5 AI Knowledge Hub Education-first, structured learning Medium

Implementation Notes

Recommended Approach

Combine Concepts 1 and 4 for the optimal experience: Start with the AI Card (Concept 1) for the initial greeting, then show question bubbles (Concept 4) as the primary interaction method. This balances guided discovery with playful engagement.

Accessibility

All interactive elements should have minimum 44x44pt touch targets. Use semantic HTML, proper ARIA labels, and ensure color contrast ratios meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards (4.5:1 for text).

Performance

Lazy-load AI responses. Use skeleton screens during loading. Cache common responses locally. Prefetch likely follow-up questions. Target less than 100ms perceived response time for pre-cached answers.

Fallback Behavior

If AI service is unavailable, show cached financial education content. Never show a blank or error state during the 5-8 minute setup window. Always provide value.
