Why Concept 2: Summary → Dedicated Screen

Concept 2 strikes the ideal balance between at-a-glance clarity and depth on demand. It follows the progressive disclosure principle — users get the numbers they need immediately, then tap to drill into details when they want them.

Key reasons this approach wins:

  • Information hierarchy — The compact summary card answers "how's my cash flow?" in under 2 seconds without overwhelming the user.
  • Progressive disclosure — The dedicated review screen with tabs (All / Income / Expenses / Changed) lets users filter and focus on what matters to them.
  • Expandable rows — Each item can reveal history, trend charts, or edit options without cluttering the main view.
  • Scalability — Works whether a user has 5 recurring items or 50. The compact card stays the same; the review screen adapts.
  • Changed items highlighted — Red badges on modified items draw attention without interrupting the flow.
  • Familiar navigation pattern — The back arrow + tab bar is a proven mobile pattern that requires zero learning curve.

When to use the others:

  • Concept 1 — Good for simple apps with <8 recurring items where full visibility is preferred.
  • Concept 3 — Best for educational or onboarding contexts where you want to teach users about cash flow.
  • Concept 4 — Ideal for quarterly or annual review flows where completion tracking is the primary goal.
  • Concept 5 — Excellent as an AI-powered feature layer on top of Concept 2's structure for premium users.
