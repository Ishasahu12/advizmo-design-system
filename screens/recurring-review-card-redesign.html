Overview

Recurring Review Card Redesign

The current card treats recurring financial changes as a notification. We need to transform it into a premium, action-oriented financial review that covers complete cash flow — expenses and income.

Concept 1 Concept 2 Concept 3 Concept 4 Concept 5 Compare Pick One

Problem vs Solution

⚠ Current Design
  • Focuses only on bills and subscriptions — ignores income entirely
  • Feels like a notification, not a meaningful financial event
  • No visibility into total financial impact (net cash flow)
  • Users don't see how changes affect their monthly budget
  • Labels changes as "bills" — excludes income and new recurring items
✓ New Design Goals
  • Complete recurring cash flow: expenses + income in one view
  • Premium, trust-inspiring financial review — not a notification
  • Shows net impact and total monthly cash flow at a glance
  • Highlights what changed and why it matters to the user
  • Supports income tracking alongside expenses

Terminology Shift

"Subscriptions changed"→"Your money flow changed"
"Review bills"→"Review your cash flow"
"6 subscriptions changed"→"3 expenses increased, 1 new subscription"
"Stay on top of spending"→"Keep your cash flow accurate"

Separate recurring updates into logical groups

Recommendation

Which Concept to Ship

Based on the analysis above, here's our recommendation for the best approach.

★ Recommended: Concept 1 + Concept 5 Hybrid

Combine Concept 1's structured data layout (totals, badges, rows) with Concept 5's conversational tone and contextual insights. This gives you the best of both worlds: fast scannability and intelligent guidance.

The card leads with the AI greeting ("Hi James, I found changes..."), then presents the structured summary (expenses, income, net) and top changes with badges. This creates a premium, trustworthy experience that's still fast to scan.

Implementation: Use the C1 card structure as the base. Add C5's AI greeting header and contextual insight cards for the top 2-3 changes. Keep the C1 summary row (Expenses | Income | Net) and badge system for individual items.

When to Use Each Concept

C1: Cash Flow Summary

Use when you need maximum information density on the homepage. Best for power users who check their finances daily and want a complete picture without tapping.

C2: Review Summary

Use for minimal, clean interfaces. Best when the card is one of many on a busy homepage and needs to be lightweight. Good for users who want awareness, not detail.

C3: Priority Updates

Use when you want to reduce noise and only surface high-impact changes. Best for users who might ignore a full list but will act on the most important change.

C4: Grouped Review

Use when your user base has diverse financial complexity. Grouping helps users find what matters to them. Best for apps that support both simple and complex financial lives.

C5: Financial Assistant

Use when positioning Advizmo as a premium, AI-powered financial companion. Best for users who want guidance and trust-building, not just raw data. Strongest brand impression.

C1+C5 Hybrid

Recommended for production. Combines data completeness with intelligent presentation. Covers all user types: power users get the data, casual users get the guidance.

Writing Principles

Do
  • Lead with the financial impact, not the notification
  • Use specific numbers ($47.50, not "some amount")
  • Reference the user's context ("your electricity")
  • Explain why it matters ("this is your largest increase")
  • Use "cash flow" instead of "bills" or "subscriptions"
Don't
  • Use passive voice ("bills were updated")
  • Be vague ("you have changes")
  • Sound like a system alert ("Notification: 4 items")
  • Ignore income ("you have new bills")
  • Use jargon ("recurring debit adjustments")
