How It's Calculated

Questions? I've got answers.

Here are the most common things people ask about how Advizmo manages their money.

Q
Why did you pick this portfolio for me?
Answer
We matched your portfolio to your risk tolerance and time horizon. When you signed up, we asked how you'd feel if your portfolio dropped 20% in a month. Your answer told us exactly how much volatility you can handle.
You said: "I'd be uncomfortable but wouldn't sell"
We chose: Balanced portfolio — 60% stocks, 40% bonds
Why: Enough growth to build wealth, enough stability to sleep well
Your situation
28 years old, investing for retirement, comfortable with moderate ups and downs. This portfolio is designed to grow steadily over 30+ years.
Q
Why do you rebalance my portfolio?
Answer
Because markets change your allocation without your permission. If stocks go up faster than bonds, you end up with more risk than you signed up for. Rebalancing keeps you on track.
Last month: US Stocks drifted from 58% to 62%
What we did: Sold 4% of US Stocks, bought International and Bonds
Result: Back to 58% target. Same risk level as before.
Rebalancing often improves returns while reducing risk — it's a win-win.
Q
Why don't you invest my money immediately?
Answer
Because we want you to get the best possible price. When the market is closed, prices aren't real — they're estimates. We wait until market open so you get actual market prices.
After hours: Prices can be unreliable, spreads are wider
Market open: 9:30 AM ET — real prices, real trading
What happens: We buy at the best available price within the first 30 minutes
Example
Your $520 Auto Invest was scheduled for Friday at 3 PM. Since markets were closed, we invested it Monday at 9:30 AM when markets opened. You got fair prices, not estimates.
Q
How does tax-loss harvesting actually save me money?
Answer
When an investment drops in value, we sell it and immediately buy a similar (not identical) fund. This locks in a loss that reduces your taxes — while you keep the same market exposure.
Step 1: International ETF dropped $340
Step 2: Sell it, buy a similar international fund
Step 3: $340 loss reduces your taxable income
Step 4: Estimated tax savings: $82
You still own international stocks — just a different fund. Same exposure, lower taxes.
Q
Why don't you invest all my cash?
Answer
Because life happens. We keep a buffer for emergencies and upcoming bills. This way you never have to sell investments at a bad time to cover unexpected expenses.
Emergency reserve: 3-6 months of expenses — always liquid
Bill buffer: Upcoming payments — kept until due
Investable: Everything else — goes to work for you
The math
Your idle cash earns 4.1% APY. That's $410/year on $10,000 — while it waits to be invested or used for emergencies. Much better than a regular savings account at 0.5%.
Q
How do I know you're not charging hidden fees?
Answer
We're completely transparent. Every fee is shown in your account. We use low-cost index funds with expense ratios as low as 0.03%. Here's what you actually pay:
Advizmo fee: Included in your subscription
ETF expense ratios: 0.03% – 0.10% annually
Trading commissions: $0
Account fees: $0
On $10,000 invested, you pay approximately $5-10 per year in fund fees. That's it.
